## BAMOCAR Performance
<p style='text-align: justify'>
The team analysed <b>BAMOCAR D3 400-400</b>'s competition acceleration performance based on data extracted using the <b>Energy Meter</b>.
</p>

<center><img src='./Figures/R25E Competition Acceleration Data.png'></center>
<center><i>Figure 6: R25E Competition Acceleration Data</i></center>

<br>

<p style='text-align: justify'>
The data revealed a peak current of 234.6A, peak power of 66.32kW, a current ramp rate of 0.39A/ms and an acceleration time of 4.271s. The team concluded there was potential to reach maximum current earlier to achieve higher top speed, evidenced by peak power being lower than expected (rule limit: 80kW, R25E limit: 72.6kW) and tractive voltage continuing to increase after tractive current peaked.
</p>

## Transition to CM200DX
<p style='text-align: justify'>
Due to <b>BAMOCAR</b>'s limited current ramp rate, the team decided to use <b>CM200DX</b> for R26E due to its superior performance. For detailed justification, refer to <a href='https://niikhilesh.github.io/System-Integration-and-Optimization-of-the-CM200DX/' target='_blank'>FTS 435 Cascadia System Integration (HV & LV)</a>.
</p>
